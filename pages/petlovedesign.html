Research Insights and Inspirations

According to a 2023 study by Pet Care Industry Trends, over 67% of households in the US own a pet, and the demand for trusted information and services is growing. The global pet care market is expected to reach $325 billion by 2028, showcasing the need for streamlined platforms.

Social media shows a rise in pet-related communities, yet lacks structure and verified resources.

Inspirations:

  • The Dodo - For emotionally engaging rescue stories.
  • Petfinder - A great platform for adoption listings.
  • GoodRx Pets - Reliable source of pet medications and tips.
  • Reddit (r/pets) - Reflects real concerns of pet owners: behavioral help, vet costs, nutrition, etc.

Target User Segments. Representative Sample.

These user segments were identified through initial market research and observation of common trends in pet ownership and care communities by analyzing forums, social media groups, and existing pet platforms.

Group Description
New pet owners People who recently adopted or bought a pet.
Long-time pet parents Experienced caretakers of dogs, cats, or exotic pets.
Animal lovers w/o pets People interested in rescue, volunteering, or future adoption.
Pet business reps From pet food, vet clinics, or shelters.
Young adults (18-30) Socially active, digitally native pet lovers.
Families with kids Parents looking for safe, educational pet platforms.

These diverse user segments form the foundation of the petl♥ve community, each bringing unique needs, experiences, and motivations. Understanding their behaviors and expectations helps tailor features, content, and support to create a truly inclusive and helpful platform for all pet lovers.

  • Confusion about pet laws and care standards

Defining the Problem

In the modern digital space, pet lovers face fragmented resources:

  • 1

    News about animal laws or medical advice is hard to find in one place and at a needed time.

  • 2

    Adoptions are widely scattered across different platforms, and forums.

  • 3

    Rescue missions and ways to support them are often invisible to the general public.

  • 4

    Pet owners need guidance on feeding, care, and behavior management, but must visit multiple sources.

  • 5

    New pet parents are often overwhelmed by information overload or misinformation.

petl♥ve centralizes all of this — creating a unified, reliable, and emotional space for users to stay informed, act compassionately, and connect meaningfully.

Name

The logo reflects the core mission: helping people connect with and care for animals, not just through information and commerce, but with love and empathy .

The heart acts as a universal symbol of compassion.

Lowercase lettering makes the brand feel approachable and friendly.

petl♥ve combines simplicity and sentiment. The heart (♥) replaces the "o" to symbolize affection, care, and emotional bonding between humans and pets.

Prototype

The prototype of the petl♥ve App is designed as a vibrant and inclusive platform that brings together pet enthusiasts, caregivers, and rescuers under one digital roof. With a warm, accessible interface and a focus on compassion-driven functionality, petl♥ve offers users the ability to:

  • Register and create pet profiles
  • Read curated news about pet medicine, animal laws, rescue efforts, pet care, and global pet-related stories
  • Adopt or buy pets responsibly
  • Find and support animal rescue efforts
  • Browse and connect with pet food companies
  • Add personal pets with photos, health logs, and more

The app aims to be a hub for both emotional connection and practical support, combining a news feed with community features and resources. The layout encourages smooth navigation, optimized both for desktop and mobile users. petl♥ve is built for pet lovers of all kinds — whether you're a first-time dog owner, a lifelong cat guardian, or a passionate animal rights advocate.
