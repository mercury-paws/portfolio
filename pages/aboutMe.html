about me

about me section

when I was 11, I asked my mother, why do we live? This question has followed me ever since.

Have you ever asked yourself the same?

I strive to understand people, to explain. But I struggle to forgive thoughtlessness. Too often, minds are consumed by self-preservation, acting out of ego rather than awareness.

I seek those who want to live with purpose, to change, to learn, and to understand. I want to help express ideas, exchange knowledge, and share voices with the world. If you resonate with this, with me and my personality shown below, or have a vision that can make a difference, reach out. Let's bring ideas to life.

my interests section

ABOUT ME

Yoga
I have been practicing yoga for 8 years already. It's a practice of strength, flexibility, and mindfulness that nourishes both body and soul.
Vegetarian
Being vegetarian is my way of respecting life. I am human, and I have a mind—thus, I bear a greater responsibility. I have a choice. I refuse to be part of the constant killing, agony, and destruction that humans bring to other species. I am not above any other animal.
Nature
In its silence, I find clarity; in its vastness, I see my place in the world. Respecting and preserving it is not a choice—it's a responsibility. Nature is the purest form ideal and wisdom. We are just one part of a vast, interconnected world. We are all made from stars, our body is the credit taken from the earth.
Psychology
My work in logistics left a void in me. The ego-driven nature of business is something people must move beyond. But to change, one must first fall—how far, though, is the question. Seeking answers, I pursued a second degree—a Bachelor's in Psychology—to understand more.
Reading
"I am fond of reading"—the first sentence I wrote in English back in fifth grade. Nothing has changed since then. Books open worlds, deepen understanding, and shape perspectives, offering endless knowledge and a quiet refuge from the noise of life.
Hiking
Walking through forests and over hills clears the mind, strengthens the body, and reminds me how small yet connected we are to nature.
Astrology
My fascination with astrology began when I was young, curious about the stars and their influence, about work of Tarot. All of it has only deepened since then. Astrology is more than just a belief—it's a way to understand oneself and others, deep psychological tool to gain insight into life's cycles and how we are all connected to the universe.
